Fundamentals of clustering

similarity or distance

A training set which has n samples and m features can be presented as an M*N matrix X.

xij means the value of i th feature of the j th sample.

Minkowski distance

$$
Minkowski\ distance\
d_{ij}=(\sum^{m}{k=1}|x{ki}-x_{kj}|^{p})^{\frac{1}{p}},p ≥1.
$$

Minkowski distance presents the distance of xi and xj.

p=2, it is Euclidean distance.p=1, it is Manhattan distance.p=∞, it is Chebyshev distance.
$$
Chebyshev\ distance\
d_{ij}=max_{k}|x_{ki}-x_{kj}|
$$

Mahalanobis distance

It’s an extension of Euclidean distance:
$$
Mahalanobis\ distance\
d_{ij}=[(x_{i}-x_{j})^{T}S^{-1}(x_{i}-x_{j})]^{\frac{1}{2}}
$$
S is the covariance matrix of X.

Correlation coefficient

The absolute value of correlation coefficient
$$
r_{ij}=\frac{\sum_{k=1}^{m}(x_{ki}-\overline{x}{i})(x{kj}-\overline{x}{j}))}{[\sum{k=1}^{m}(x_{ki}-\overline{x}{i})^2\sum{k=1}^{m}(x_{kj}-\overline{x}_{j})^2]^{\frac{1}{2}}}
$$
The more it is close to 1, the more similar i and j are, the more it is close to 0, the more unsimilar they are.

Cosine

$$
s_{ij}=\frac{\sum_{k=1}^{m}x_{ki}x_{kj}}{[\sum_{k=1}^{m}x_{ki}^2\sum_{k=1}^{m}x_{kj}^2]^{\frac{1}{2}}}
$$
The more cosine is close to 1, the more similar i and j are, the more it is close to 0, the more unsimilar they are.

Monte Carlo method

random sampling

The point of Monte Carlo method is random sampling, one of Monte Carlo method is accept-reject sampling method.

algorithm is as follow :

Input : probabilistic density function p(x) of sampling target,

Output : random samples x1,x2,…,xn of probabilistic distribution,

Parameter : the number of samples n,

step 1: choose probabilistic density function q(x) as advised distribution, make it satisfy cq(x)≥p(x) for all x, where c>0,

step 2 : random sampling from q(x) get sample x*, then sampling from uniform distribution (0,1) get sample u,

step 3 : if u satisfy :
$$
u≤\frac{p(x*)}{cq(x*)}
$$
then x* is the result of sampling, or go back to step 2,

step 4 : over it when we get n random samples.

The upside of the method is it is easy, the downside of the method is it is not efficient enough.

estimation of mathematical expectation

To calculate the mathematical expectation Ep(x)[f(x)] of f(x) about probabilistic function p(x) :
$$
E_{p(x)}[f(x)]\approx \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^nf(x_i)
$$

Monte Carlo integration

Decompose h(x) to f(x) and density function p(x), we have :
$$
\int_{\chi}h(x)dx=\int_{\chi}f(x)p(x)dx=E_{p(x)}[f(x)]
$$
The samples of Organized Monte Carlo are independent, but the samples of Markov Chain Monte Carlo method are independent.

Monte Carlo method

random sampling

The point of Monte Carlo method is random sampling, one of Monte Carlo method is accept-reject sampling method.

algorithm is as follow :

Input : probabilistic density function p(x) of sampling target,

Output : random samples x1,x2,‚Ä¶,xn of probabilistic distribution,

Parameter : the number of samples n,

step 1: choose probabilistic density function q(x) as advised distribution, make it satisfy cq(x)‚â•p(x) for all x, where c>0,

step 2 : random sampling from q(x) get sample x*, then sampling from uniform distribution (0,1) get sample u,

step 3 : if u satisfy :
$$
u‚â§\frac{p(x*)}{cq(x*)}
$$
then x* is the result of sampling, or go back to step 2,

step 4 : over it when we get n random samples.

The upside of the method is it is easy, the downside of the method is it is not efficient enough.

estimation of mathematical expectation

To calculate the mathematical expectation Ep(x)[f(x)] of f(x) about probabilistic function p(x) :
$$
E_{p(x)}[f(x)]\approx \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^nf(x_i)
$$

Monte Carlo integration

Decompose h(x) to f(x) and density function p(x), we have :
$$
\int_{\chi}h(x)dx=\int_{\chi}f(x)p(x)dx=E_{p(x)}[f(x)]
$$
The samples of Organized Monte Carlo are independent, but the samples of Markov Chain Monte Carlo method are independent.

Markov chain

basic definition

The direct definition of Markov property is ‚ÄúThe future only depend on the present and has nothing to do with the past‚Äù. If state movement distribution P(Xt|Xt-1) doesn‚Äôt rely on t, then
$$
P(X_{t+s}|X_{t+s-1})=P(X_t|X_{t-1})
$$
this markov chain is time homogenous Markov chain.

discrete state markov chain

Markov chain is in state j in time (t-1), move to state i in time t, notate probability of movement as :
$$
p_{ij}=(X_t=i|X_{t-1}=j),i=1,2,‚Ä¶;j=1,2,‚Ä¶
$$
satisfy
$$
p_{ij}‚â•0,\sum_{i}p_{ij}=1
$$
We can use markov chain in NLP and speech processing and so on, we consider a sentence as a sequence of word w1w2‚Ä¶ws, then we can get :
$$
P(w_1w_2‚Ä¶w_s)=P(w_1)P(w_2|w_1)‚Ä¶P(w_i|w_1w_2‚Ä¶w_{i-1})‚Ä¶P(w_s|w_1w_2‚Ä¶w_{s-1})\=P(w_1)P(w_2|w_1)P(w_3|w_2)‚Ä¶P(w_i|w_{i-1})‚Ä¶P(w_s|w_{s-1})
$$
Have learned smooth distribution before, so the only thing I wanna say here is that markov chain can have one smooth distribution or ifinite smooth distribution or no smooth distribution.

continuous state markov chain

we have
$$
P(X_t=A|X_{t-1}=x)=P(x,A)=\int_Ap(x,y)dy
$$
where p(x,y) is a probabilistic density function and it satisfies
$$
p(x,y)‚â•0,P(x,S)=\int_Sp(x,y)dy=1.
$$

properties of Markov chain

1.irreducible
$$
P(X_t=i|X_0=j)>0
$$
an irreducible markov chain can achieve any state from random state if time is enough.

2.aperiodic

the GCD of {t:P(Xt=i|X0=i)>0} is 1, then this markov chain is aperiodic.

An irreducible and aperiodic limited state markov chain has an only smooth distribuiton.

3.positive recurrent

ptij is probability of move to i the frist time in time t from j in time 0, if we have
$$
\mathop{lim}\limits_{t\rightarrow \infin}p^t_{ij}>0
$$
for any state i,j, then the markov chain is positive recurrent.

A irreducible, aperiodic and positive recurrent markov chain has an only smooth distribution

4.reversible Markov chain

for an time t, we have
$$
p_{ji}\pi_j=p_{ij}\pi_i,i,j=1,2,‚Ä¶
$$
it is called detailed balance equation.

Markov Chain Monte Carlo method

This method is more suitable for random variable is multiple, the density funciton is non-standard and so on.

basic steps

step 1 : contruct a traversal markov chain on state zone S, make its smooth distribution be target distribution p(x),

step 2 : from some point x0 of state zone, taking random walk by using constructed markov chain, get samples x0,x1,‚Ä¶,xt,‚Ä¶,

step 3 : using traversal theorem, configure positive integers m and n, (m<n), get a sample set {xm+1,xm+2,‚Ä¶,xn}, get the average value of f(x) :
$$
\hat{E}f=\frac{1}{n-m}\sum_{i=m+1}^nf(x_i)
$$
some problems here : how to definite markov chain to make the conditions be ture, how to definite m and n to ensure the no-variance of sampling and the precision of average value.

statistic learning

Markov chain Monte Carlo method is important in statistic learning, especially bayesian learning, where three kinds of integer calculation always needed to be done : normalization, marginalization and expectation as follows :
$$
\int_\chi p(y|x‚Äô)p(x‚Äô)dx‚Äô\p(x|y)=\int_Zp(x,z|y)dz\E_{P(x|y)}[f(x)]=\int_\chi f(x)p(x|y)dx

where z‚ààZ is a latent variable, when observed data and model are complex, we can use Markov chain Monte Carlo method to solve the problem.

Metropolis-Hastings

The sampling probabilistic distribution is p(x), use a Markov Chain whose convert core is p(x,x‚Äô) :
$$
p(x,x‚Äô)=q(x,x‚Äô)\alpha(x,x‚Äô)
$$
where q(x,x‚Äô) is called proposal distribution and ùù∞(x,x‚Äô) is called acceptance distribution.

Acceptance distribution is :
$$
\alpha(x,x‚Äô)=min{1,\frac{p(x‚Äô)q(x‚Äô,x)}{p(x)q(x,x‚Äô)}}
$$
write convert core p(x,x‚Äô) as :
$$
p(x,x‚Äô)=\begin{cases}q(x,x‚Äô),p(x‚Äô)q(x‚Äô,x)‚â•p(x)q(x,x‚Äô) \ q(x‚Äô,x)\frac{p(x‚Äô)}{p(x)},p(x‚Äô)q(x‚Äô,x)<p(x)q(x,x‚Äô)\end{cases}
$$
random walk as :
$$
x_t=\begin{cases}x‚Äô,u‚â§\alpha(x,x‚Äô)\x,u>\alpha(x,x‚Äô)\end{cases}
$$
There are two kinds of advised distribution are commonly used :

(1)Suppose the advised distribution is symmertrical, we have :
$$
q(x,x‚Äô)=q(x‚Äô,x)
$$
the advised distribution is called Metropolis choose, where acceptance distribution is simplified as
$$
\alpha(x,x‚Äô)=min{1,\frac{p(x‚Äô)}{p(x)}}
$$

A special example for Metropolis choose is set q(x,x‚Äô) as conditional probabilistic distribution p(x‚Äô|x), definite it as multiple variable normal distribution whose mean value is x, convariance matrix is constant matrix.

The other special example for Metropolis choose is set q(x,x‚Äô)=q(|x=x‚Äô|), the algorithm is called random walk Metropolis algorithm, such as
$$
q(x,x‚Äô)\propto e^{-\frac{(x‚Äô-x)^2}{2}}
$$
(2)Independent sampling, suppose that q(x,x‚Äô) has nothing to do with present state x, we can get q(x,x‚Äô) = q(x‚Äô), the calculation of adviced distribution will be done by q(x‚Äô), we can write acceptance distribution as
$$
\alpha(x,x‚Äô)=min{1,\frac{\omega(x‚Äô)}{\omega(x)}}
$$
where ùõö(x‚Äô)=p(x‚Äô)/q(x‚Äô),ùõö(x)=p(x)/q(x).

Metropolis-Hastings algorithm

Input : The density function of sampling target distribution, function f(x);

Output : random samples of p(x) xm+1,xm+2,‚Ä¶,xn, the mean value of samples fmn;

Parameters : convergence steps m, iteration steps n;

(1)choose x0 randomly;

(2)for i = 1,2,‚Ä¶,n do the loop :

‚Äã (a)set xi-1=x,random choose a candidate state x‚Äô by adviced distribution q(x,x‚Äô);

‚Äã (b)calculate the probability
$$
\alpha(x,x‚Äô)=min{1,\frac{p(x‚Äô)q(x‚Äô,x)}{p(x)q(x,x‚Äô)}}
$$
‚Äã (c)choose a number u from uniform distribution (0,1)

‚Äã if u‚â§ùù∞(x,x‚Äô),then state xi=x‚Äô. Otherwise, xi=x.

(3)get the sample set {xm+1,xm+1,‚Ä¶,xn}

calculate
$$
f_{mn}=\frac{1}{n-m}\sum_{i=m+1}^{n}f(x_i)

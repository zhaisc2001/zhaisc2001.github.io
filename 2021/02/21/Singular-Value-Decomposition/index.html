Singular Value Decomposition

2021-02-21 technology PV:

Definition of SVD and its properties

The singular value decomposition of a matrix is usually referred to as the SVD. This is the final and best factorization of a matrix:
$$
A=U\sum V^T
$$
where U and V are orthogonal, and ∑ is a rectangular diagonal matrix. Here, A can be any matrix, besides they satisfies:
$$
UU^T=I\VV^T=I\\sum=diag(\sigma_1,\sigma_2,…,\sigma_p)\\sigma_1≥\sigma_2≥…≥\sigma_p≥0\p=min(m,n)
$$
We call the singular value decomposition as below full SVD. We often use compact singular value decomposition and truncated singular value decomposition in practice.

Compact SVD: for a mn matrix, rank(A)=r, r≤min(m,n), its compact SVD is :
$$
A=U_r\sum\ _rV_r^T
$$
Ur is a mr matrix, ∑r is a r*r matrix, Vr is a n*r matrix. Ur is the first r lines of U, Vr is the first r lines of V, ∑ is composed with the first r dialog elements of ∑.

And truncated SVD is :
$$
A\approx U_k\sum\ _kV_k^T
$$
where rank(A) = r, 0<k<r, Uk is a mk matrix, ∑r is a k\k matrix, Vk is a n*k matrix. Uk is the first k lines of U, Vr is the first k lines of V, ∑ is composed with the first k dialog elements of ∑.

Singular value decompostion and approximation of matrix

SVD is also an approximation method under Frobenius norm. The definition of Frobenius norm is :
$$
||A||F=(\sum{i=1}^m\sum_{j=1}^n(a_{ij})^2)^{\frac{1}{2}}
$$
Actually, in SVD we have :
$$
||A||_F=||U\sum V^T||_F=||\sum||_F\=(\sigma_1^2+\sigma_2^2+…+\sigma_n^2)^{\frac{1}{2}}
$$
